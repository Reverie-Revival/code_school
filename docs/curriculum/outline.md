# Code School — Curriculum Outline (Python)

**Status: DRAFT — pending James's approval.** Table of contents only — chapter and lesson *titles*, no lesson content written yet beyond what's already built (Ch. 1, Lessons 1.1–1.2). This is the frame to react to before any more content gets written.

Audience: two boys, ages 9 and 12 — total beginners. Tone throughout: respectful, young-adult voice, heavy scaffolding at their actual skill level (see `memory/user_team.md`). Ambition: this should be as good as, or better than, a paid course — thorough coverage of fundamentals, not a bare-minimum set.

Structure per CLAUDE.md: **Chapter → Lesson → Project.** Every chapter ends with a short wrap-up/recap lesson (a lightweight review, not a big Project). Only the four checkpoint chapters (4, 8, 11, 15) get a full **Project** — a bigger, not-auto-graded piece pulling together everything so far in that chapter *and* prior chapters.

## Notes on this draft

- **Chapters 13–15 are intentionally shorter** than the 4–8 lesson range elsewhere — there's genuinely less new ground to cover at that point (Ch. 13 is a light second half of functions with its own mini-checkpoint per CLAUDE.md; Ch. 14 is a small, self-contained topic; Ch. 15 is assembly, not new concepts). Padding them with filler lessons for the sake of a consistent count would work against the "exceptional, not just complete" goal — open to discussion if this feels too thin.
- **Recap lessons are each their own numbered lesson** in this draft (e.g. 5.1, 9.1, 12.1) rather than folded into the next lesson — flagged as still open in the plan; easy to change either way.
- **Every chapter gets a "Wrap-Up" lesson**, even non-checkpoint chapters — a small end-of-chapter review, distinct from and much lighter than the four checkpoint Projects.
- Nothing here is final. Go chapter by chapter (or wherever you want to start) and tell me what to add, cut, reorder, split, or merge.
